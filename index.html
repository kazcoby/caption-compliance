Live Captioning Requirements for Online Instruction

New federal ADA Title II regulations require live captions with speaker identification for all synchronous online courses. Here's everything you need to know.

Get Started Now View Checklist

What's Changing?

The U.S. Department of Justice has issued new regulations that affect how we deliver live online instruction.

!
This is a Legal Requirement, Not a Request

Starting April 24, 2026, all live online instruction must include real-time captions with speaker identification. This applies to every synchronous online class, regardless of whether any student has requested accommodations.

CC

Proactive Captions

Captions must be available for ALL sessions, not just upon request. This is a major shift from previous policy.

ID

Speaker Identification

Captions must make clear who is speaking, especially in discussion-based courses with multiple participants.

LIVE

Real-Time Delivery

Captions must appear synchronized with live speech, not added later. This affects all live Zoom sessions.

ALL

Universal Access

Benefits everyone: deaf/hard of hearing students, ESL learners, those in noisy environments, and students who learn better with text.

Why This Matters

Legal Consequences

  • Office for Civil Rights complaints
  • Loss of federal funding
  • Legal action from students
  • Course restrictions or closure

Student Benefits

  • 98.6% of caption users say they're helpful
  • 75% use captions as a learning aid
  • 52% report improved comprehension
  • Benefits extend beyond disability accommodations

What's Required?

Understanding the federal requirements under WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards.

Affected Content
  • All synchronous online courses
  • Hybrid/HyFlex courses with live components
  • Live-streamed lectures and presentations
  • Office hours conducted via Zoom
  • Committee and department meetings (public)
  • Any live audio content accessible online
NOT Affected (Yet)
  • In-person-only classes with no online component
  • Asynchronous content (separate, existing requirements)
  • Private one-on-one meetings
  • Internal staff meetings without public access

Note: Pre-recorded videos have required captions for years - that's not new.

i
The Key Change: Proactive, Not Reactive

Previously, captions were provided "upon request" through disability accommodations. Now, captions must be proactively available for ALL students in ALL live sessions, regardless of whether anyone has requested accommodations.

How to Enable Live Captions in Zoom

Follow these steps to ensure your live classes are compliant.

Turn On Zoom Auto-Captions

This is the essential first step for every live class session.

  1. Start your Zoom meeting as usual
  2. Look for the Live Transcript or Captions button on the toolbar
  3. Click it and select Enable Auto-Transcription
  4. Captions will now appear for all participants who enable them
TIP
Students can turn captions on/off individually - they control their own viewing experience.

Update Your Zoom Display Name

Your display name helps automated captions identify you as the speaker.

  1. Before your meeting, click on your name/video
  2. Select Rename
  3. Change to include your role: "Dr. Smith - Instructor" or "Prof. Garcia"
  4. This name will appear in captions when you speak

Implement Speaker Identification Practices

Federal standards require that captions identify speakers so viewers can follow multi-speaker discussions.

When Calling on Students:

Use their name when acknowledging them:

"Thank you, Marcus. Marcus, what's your answer?"

This helps both automated captions and students following along identify who is speaking.

Repeating Student Questions:

Always repeat or paraphrase questions with the student's name:

"Thanks Sarah, you're asking about the deadline for the midterm project..."

This ensures both the question AND the speaker are captured in captions.

Why This Matters:

A hearing student can see who's raising their hand. A deaf or hard-of-hearing student using captions needs the same information. Without speaker identification, captions don't provide equivalent access.

Make It a Habit

Add caption activation to your class startup routine.

  1. Create a "Class Startup Checklist" (physical or digital)
  2. Include "Enable Live Transcript" as a mandatory item
  3. Consider adding a reminder slide to your presentation
  4. Record occasionally to review caption quality
!
Starting Spring 2026, courses without captions are out of compliance. Make it a habit now!

Best Practices for Caption Quality

Simple techniques to improve caption accuracy and readability.

Speaking Techniques
  • Speak clearly and at a moderate pace
  • Pause briefly between speakers
  • Avoid talking over others
  • Minimize background noise
  • Use a quality microphone when possible
  • Position microphone 6-12 inches from mouth
Discussion Management
  • Structure discussions: "I'm calling on Ahmed, then Lisa, then Carlos"
  • Use students' names when calling on them
  • Repeat questions with attribution
  • Summarize key points periodically
  • Manage overlapping speech
  • Use name tents or seating charts
Hybrid/HyFlex Classrooms
  • Have in-person students also join Zoom individually
  • Students can join muted with camera optional
  • When speaking, student unmutes personal device
  • Captions show student's name automatically
  • Use headphones to prevent audio feedback
When Tech Solutions Aren't Available
  • Always repeat student questions with names
  • Example: "Excellent question from Alex about cellular division..."
  • Summarize discussions: "Building on Jamie's point..."
  • Self-identify: "This is Dr. Smith speaking..."
  • Document your compliance efforts

Sample Meeting Opening Script

"Good morning/afternoon, everyone. Before we begin, I want to remind you that live captions are available for this class. You can enable them by clicking the CC or Show Captions button in your Zoom toolbar. To help ensure accurate speaker identification in the captions, please state your name before speaking, especially during discussions."
