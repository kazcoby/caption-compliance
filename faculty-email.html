Action Required: New Federal Live Captioning Requirements

Dear Colleagues,

COMPLIANCE DEADLINE: April 24, 2026
New federal ADA regulations require live captions for all synchronous online instruction

Starting Spring 2026, all faculty teaching live online courses must enable real-time captioning. This is no longer optional or provided only upon request—it's a legal requirement under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) Title II.

What You Need to Know

  • Proactive, Not Reactive: Captions must be available for ALL live sessions, regardless of whether any student has requested accommodations
  • Speaker Identification Required: Captions must make clear who is speaking, especially in discussions
  • Applies to All Live Content: Every synchronous Zoom session in your online or hybrid courses
Immediate Actions You Can Take This Week:
  1. Turn on Zoom auto-captions — Click "Live Transcript" → "Enable Auto-Transcription"
  2. Update your Zoom display name — Include your role (e.g., "Dr. Smith - Instructor")
  3. Use student names — When calling on students, say their names clearly
  4. Repeat questions — "Thanks Sarah, you're asking about the deadline..."

Why This Matters

Beyond legal compliance, captions benefit ALL students. Research shows:

  • 98.6% of students who use captions say they're helpful
  • 75% use captions as a learning aid
  • 52% report improved comprehension

Captions help deaf/hard of hearing students, ESL learners, students in noisy environments, and those who learn better with text reinforcement.

Support Available

For Spring 2026, the college is providing enhanced captioning services through the California DECT Grant, which covers live captioning for courses that need additional support beyond Zoom's auto-captions.
